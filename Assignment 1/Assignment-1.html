Introduction

Over the last quarter, our online retail company implemented a personalized marketing campaign to drive sales and customer engagement. To optimize future budgets, we need to understand the return on investment for our targeted outreach efforts. This report investigates the relationship between the financial resources allocated to individual customers—through ads, emails, and coupons—and their subsequent purchasing behavior. Ultimately, these insights will guide our strategic decisions on whether increasing marketing spend per customer translates into meaningful revenue growth.

Research Question

This analysis seeks to answer a single, critical question regarding our recent campaign: did higher marketing spend per customer lead to higher overall purchase amounts? By treating the quarter’s campaign data as the canonical record, we will determine if there is a positive financial correlation between individual marketing investments and customer revenue

Dataset Introduction

The dataset serves as the definitive record of our personalized marketing campaign over the previous quarter, recording customer identifiers, sign-up dates, engagement metrics, and financial data. To prepare this data for analysis, a rigorous cleaning process was undertaken. First, we standardized the highly irregular customer sign-up dates into a uniform date structure. Next, we removed any records that contained missing values in the critical marketing spend or purchase amount columns. Finally, we filtered the data to ensure only valid, non-negative financial values were included, resulting in a reliable foundation for evaluating campaign performance.

Dataset Description

The cleaned dataset consists of 150 observations and 7 variables, representing individual customers engaged during the campaign. The dataset encompasses multiple variable types that provide a comprehensive view of customer behavior. Specifically, it includes continuous numeric variables tracking financial metrics and engagement, categorical variables defining market groups (segment), and standardized datetime structures for registration.

To provide broader context for our analysis, it is essential to understand the composition of our customer base.
